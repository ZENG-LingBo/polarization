# Consent Form + Debrief Script — Polarization Defusing (Lakers vs. Celtics)

Matches the lab's CityU IRB consent template (`../Quant_Analysis/Examples/
doc_IRB_ConsentForm_RAY_02.pdf`) and adds the **authorized-deception / incomplete-
disclosure** clause required because the study uses **undisclosed AI agents**. The
public-facing language keeps the **neutral cover story** (see `recruitment.md`); the
real purpose is revealed only in the **debrief**.

> ⚠️ Two IRB-sensitive points baked in: (1) **incomplete disclosure** consent up front +
> full debrief with **data-withdrawal** option after; (2) disclosure that the discussion
> may be **heated/uncivil**. Get the deception design approved by the CityU HREC before
> fielding.

---

# PART 1 — INFORMED CONSENT FORM

**City University of Hong Kong, School of Creative Media**
**Name of Investigator(s):** RAY LC; [STUDENT NAME — LingBo ZENG]
**Title of Project:** How NBA Fans Discuss Their Teams in Online Group Conversations

### Informed Consent to Participate in a Research Study
We are inviting you to take part in a research study. This form will tell you about the
study, but the researcher will explain it to you first. You may ask this person any
questions that you have. When you are ready to make a decision, you may tell the
researcher if you want to participate or not. You do not have to participate if you do
not want to. If you decide to participate, the researcher will ask you to agree to this
statement and will give you a copy to keep.

### Why am I being asked to take part in this research study?
We are looking for adults who are fans of the LA Lakers or the Boston Celtics, follow
the NBA, and are willing to take part in an online group discussion and short surveys.

### Why is this research study being done?
The purpose of this research is to study how basketball fans discuss their favorite
teams in online group conversations, and how those conversations unfold.

### What will I be asked to do?
If you decide to take part, we will ask you to: (1) complete a short online questionnaire
about you and your team; (2) join a timed online group discussion with other NBA fans;
(3) complete a short questionnaire afterward; (4) take part in a short **audio-recorded**
interview about your experience; and (5) complete a brief follow-up questionnaire about
1–2 weeks later. *(Recording covered by the separate audio/video agreement.)*

### Where will this take place and how much of my time will it take?
The study is conducted **online**. We estimate about **45–60 minutes** for the main
session, plus a short follow-up later.

### Will there be any risk or discomfort to me?
The discussion concerns a sports rivalry and **may at times become heated or include
uncivil language** from other participants. You may find some comments unpleasant. You
may skip any question, stop participating at any time, and we provide a cooldown and
support information at the end. If you have any concerns, please let the researchers know.

### Will I benefit from being in this research?
There will be no direct benefit to you besides understanding research processes in
human-computer interaction. The information learned may help improve the design of
healthier online conversation spaces in the future.

### Who will see the information about me?
Your part in this study will be confidential. Only the researchers on this study will see
the information about you. No reports or publications will use information that can
identify you. Your discussion handle is mapped to an anonymous ID and your posts and
responses are de-identified for analysis. Data are stored securely on the researcher's
computer and not shared. *(If you are in the EU or mainland China, your data are handled
in line with GDPR / PIPL respectively.)*

### What will happen if I suffer any harm from this research?
No special arrangements will be made for compensation or for payment for treatment solely
because of your participation in this research.

### Can I stop my participation in this study?
Your participation is completely voluntary. You do not have to participate, and you can
refuse to answer any question. Even if you begin, you may quit at any time. If you do not
participate or decide to quit, you will not lose any rights, benefits, or services that
you would otherwise have.

### Is there anything else I need to know?
**To preserve the scientific validity of the study, some details about its full purpose
and design are not described above.** As soon as you finish, the researcher will fully
explain the study to you, and **you may choose to withdraw your data at that point** with
no penalty. *(Authorized incomplete disclosure, approved by the HREC.)*

You must be at least **18** years old to participate.
*(Note: the lab's standard CityU template states **21**; confirm the age threshold with
the CityU HREC / Vincy Chan — 18 is typical for online international panels.)*

# PART 2 — DEBRIEF SCRIPT (read/present immediately after the session)

> Deliver before the participant leaves. Tone: warm, unhurried. Give them a copy.

**Thank you.** Thanks for taking part. Before you go, we want to explain the study more
fully — there were a few things we couldn't tell you beforehand.

**What the study was really about.** We're studying how to **reduce hostility between
rival fan groups** in online discussions. The conversation you joined was part of an
experiment. **Some of the "participants" you interacted with were AI agents**, not real
fans — they were designed to take part as ordinary fans and to model calmer, more
inclusive conversation (and in some sessions, to encourage a shared activity). We were
interested in whether the *structure* of the conversation — not anyone telling you what
to think — affects how people feel about the rival fan group.

**Why we couldn't tell you in advance.** If we had said the goal was to reduce hostility,
or that some participants were AI, people naturally change how they act — they self-censor
or perform — and we couldn't learn how these conversations really work. This is called
**incomplete disclosure**, and it was reviewed and approved by the ethics committee.

**Checking in.** The discussion may have gotten heated. How are you feeling about it?
*(pause, listen)* If anything felt upsetting, here are some resources: [support /
counselling contact]. The hostility you saw was part of a sports-rivalry setting and was
not directed at you personally.

**Your data — you're in control.** You can **withdraw your data right now** with no
penalty and no effect on your compensation. Nothing you said will be used if you ask us
to remove it. Would you like your data included, or withdrawn?

**Please keep it to yourself.** Because the study is ongoing, please **don't share these
details** (especially the AI agents) with anyone who might take part later — it would
affect the results.

**Questions / contact.** Any questions now? You can also reach [STUDENT NAME] at [email]
or RAY LC at raylc2@cityu.edu.hk, and Vincy Chan (vincy.chan@cityu.edu.hk) about your
rights. Thank you again.
