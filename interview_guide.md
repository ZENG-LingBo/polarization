# Post-Session Interview Guide — Polarization Defusing (Lakers vs. Celtics)

Semi-structured, ~15–20 min, run right after the platform session, before full debrief.
Built to the lab's interview rules (Ray, HCIX): **ask more *how* than *why*; no leading
questions; let people finish; use plain language; ask for clarification but don't impose
your interpretation; don't judge; don't ask about something specific until they mention
it.** Record with consent.

> **Critical non-leading rule for this study:** do **not** mention "AI / bots,"
> "polarization," "intervention," or "common identity" until/unless the participant
> raises it. Agent-detection and common-identity must surface *unprompted* to be valid.
> If they never mention the agents, that itself is data.

---

## 0. Warm-up (rapport, identity salience)
- Tell me a bit about being a [TEAM] fan — how long, how into it are you?
- How would your friends describe you as a fan?

## 1. The experience (RQ1 — process/felt experience)
- Walk me through what that discussion was like for you. *(let them narrate)*
- How did the tone feel over the session — did it change at any point? *(probe: how?)*
- Tell me about a moment that stood out — heated, or surprising, or anything.
- How did you decide when to jump in or hold back?

## 2. Their own behavior (RQ1)
- How would you describe the way *you* posted in there?
- Were there moments you wanted to fire back but didn't? What was going on there?
- *(if toxicity arose)* How did it feel when things got heated?

## 3. The other participants (RQ3 mechanism — agent detection, **unprompted**)
- What stood out to you about the other people in the thread?
- Did anyone change how the conversation was going? Tell me how. *(don't name agents)*
- *(only if they mention someone calm/odd/bot-like)* What gave you that impression?

## 4. Common ground (RQ3 — common-identity discovery, **let it surface**)
- Did anything during the session feel like you had something in common with the
  [RIVAL] fans? *(probe: when did that come up — how?)*
- *(if a shared task happened)* How was working on that together with [RIVAL] fans?
- Did the way you think about [RIVAL] fans shift at all during the session? How?

## 5. Closing
- If you ran this space, what would you change to make the conversation better?
- Anything about the session you keep thinking about?
- Anything I should have asked but didn't?

→ Then proceed to **debrief** (reveal study purpose + agents, offer data withdrawal).

---

## Probes (keep handy)
"How do you mean?" · "Can you walk me through that?" · "What happened right before
that?" · "What did that feel like?" · *(silence — let them fill it)*

## Note for the interviewer
Keep your script short, look at the person not your notes, leave room for their
questions. Tag the recording/transcript by `P#`; codes go into the Stream A open-coding
sheet same day (see `qualitative_workflow.md`).
